Energy Condensation¶

In order to obtain the few-group cross-sections we need to collapse the \(\sigma(E)\) with the flux \(\phi(E)\). To find the few-group cross sections we use the energy continues diffusion equation (1).

(1)¶\[\begin{split}&\frac{1}{v}\frac{\partial \phi}{\partial t}= \nabla \cdot D\nabla\phi-\Sigma_{a}\phi-\Sigma_{s}\phi \\ & + \int_{0}^{\infty}\Sigma_{s}(E' \rightarrow E)\phi (E')dE' \\ &+\chi(E)\int_{0}^{\infty}\nu(E')\Sigma_{f}(E')\phi(E')\end{split}\]

Please note that \(\phi=\phi(\mathbf{r},E,t)\). The group flux is defined as:

(2)¶\[\phi_g(\mathbf{r},t)\equiv \int_{E_g}^{E_{g-1}}\phi(\mathbf{r},E,t)dE\]

We will now eliminate the energy variable in the energy-dependent diffusion by integrating Eq. (1) over the \(g^{th}\) energy group. These will generate rigorous definition of group-averaged cross sections.

The absorption (3) and production (4) cross sections are straightforward to calculate. The scattering removal term has the same form, however, the scattering (source) term requires more work as described in Eq. (5).

(3)¶\[\Sigma_{ag} = \frac{1}{\phi_g}\int_{E_g}^{E_{g-1}}\Sigma_a(E) \phi(E)dE\]
(4)¶\[\begin{split}& \nu_{g'}\Sigma_{fg'} \equiv \frac{1}{\phi_g}\int_{E_g}^{E_{g-1}}\nu(E') \Sigma_f(E') \phi(E') dE' \\ & \chi_g \equiv \int_{E_g}^{E_{g-1}} \chi(E)dE\end{split}\]
(5)¶\[\Sigma_{sg'g}\equiv \frac{1}{\phi_g'}\int_{E_g}^{E_{g-1}}dE\int_{E_{g'}}^{E_{g'-1}} \Sigma_s(E'\rightarrow E) \phi(E') dE'\]

The group-wise diffusion coefficient is evaluated using the \(\nabla \phi\) rather than the flux values:

(6)¶\[D_g=\frac{\int_{E_g}^{E_{g-1}}D(E) \nabla \phi(E)dE}{\int_{E_g}^{E_{g-1}}\nabla \phi(E)dE}\]

and the neutron speed characterizing group g is:

(7)¶\[\frac{1}{v_g}\equiv\frac{1}{\phi_g}\int_{E_g}^{E_{g-1}}\frac{1}{v(E)}\phi(E)dE\]
